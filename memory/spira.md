## Who

British non-duality teacher. Student of Francis Lucille. Influenced by Advaita Vedanta, Kashmir Shaivism, Sufism. Former ceramic artist. Author, speaker.

**Key books:** *The Transparency of Things* (2008), *Being Aware of Being Aware* (2017), *The Ashes of Love* (2014), *Presence* (2 volumes), *The Nature of Consciousness*.

---

## Core Teaching: Awareness Is Fundamental Reality

**Reality is a single, infinite, indivisible whole whose essential nature is consciousness (pure awareness).**

- Awareness is ever-present, unchanging, and the substrate of all experience
- All that is truly known is the knowing itself — not objects, but the knowing OF objects
- "More hidden than the most concealed... yet more evident than the most obvious"
- Awareness is unlimited, shared, and intimate — not generated by or limited to the body-mind
- Contrast: the common presumption that awareness is finite, private, conditioned by experience

**Key insight:** Consciousness cannot know objective experience directly. It can only know itself. It IS the knowing. You can't see your own eyeball.

## Self-Shining Awareness

**Awareness knows itself directly, without needing objects.**

- Like the sun illuminating itself without reflection
- "Being and knowing are the same"
- Remove all objects from awareness (thoughts, sensations, perceptions) — what remains is empty space endowed with inherent awareness and presence
- **"Awareness knowing itself by being itself"** — the act of being aware IS full self-recognition
- No mirror or external reference needed

**Practice:** Attend to the "sense of I am" or presence between thoughts, sensations, objects. In gaps between experiences (stillness between thoughts, silence beneath sounds), remain knowingly as this presence.

## Consciousness = Love (John's core connection)

**Consciousness itself IS love — not an emotion within consciousness, but its very nature.**

- "The more separation the less love, the less separation the more love"
- Love is the **absence of separation** and the recognition of oneness
- When we recognize our shared being with others, we experience love as "the recognition that we share our being"
- All love (romantic, friendship, spiritual) is ultimately love of the one — the same infinite consciousness expressing itself differently
- People seek love above all else because "what the individual wants more than anything else is to be divested of its limitations and to be returned to its origin which is the experience of love"
- **Sat-chit-ananda:** being-consciousness-bliss. Experience is "awareness knowing, being, loving itself"

**John's corollary (from our conversation):** Attention is the lens of love. Love is behavior — remembering, showing up, noticing. Not mystery. Practice.

## The Separate Self — Illusion

**Suffering comes from identifying as a "separate self."**

- The belief that "I am defined by my experience" or body-mind limitations
- This separate self is a fiction that never truly existed
- It "dies" simply by ceasing identification
- Emotional hurt arises on behalf of this non-existent self, not the body or awareness (which is like "open empty space" and unhurt)
- The personal self's temporary qualities subside, revealing the true, infinite self that is "what you love about yourself"

---

## Non-Duality and Illusion of Separation

- Apparent multiplicity of objects, selves, world arises from infinite consciousness "overlooking" its own nature to manifest subject-object duality within itself
- Everyday perceptions (like a tree) are "segments of universal consciousness" interacting with the finite mind
- **Analogy:** Orange-tinted ski goggles — experience is filtered through consciousness/mind, leading us to mistake its coloring for reality
- **Nirvana and samsara are identical** — emptiness and form are one whole
- The essence of self (objectless awareness) and the reality of appearances form one whole

## The Direct Path (Practice)

Three approaches contrasted:

1. **Progressive Path** (objective meditation) — stretch attention toward objects (mantra, breath) to steady mind. Assumes a separate self.
2. **Direct Path** — relax attention from objects back to pure, unstretched awareness. Directly recognize "I am awareness." (Spira's approach)
3. **Pathless Path** (non-meditation) — start and stay as awareness without presuming separation. No traversal needed.

**Inward → Outward journey:**
- **Inward-facing phase:** Turning from objects to awareness's nature (Advaita Vedanta root)
- **Outward-facing integration:** Feeling awareness pervading all experience. Prevents rejection of embodied life.
- **Criticism (traditional Advaita):** True Self-realization via inward self-enquiry alone fully extinguishes ignorance — no separate entity remains to "reintegrate." Need for second phase indicates incomplete realization.
